  • My Values
  • November 20 2014



Accomplishment Achievement Competition Confidence Honesty

Achievement - I picked this value because not only the feeling but the word itself stands out to me personally. When thinking back on the times I have felt happiest, it was at a time of achievement. I have been through several trying times in my life and being to overcome my struggles has made me the person I am today. Being recognized for achievements is not necessarily as important as the personal feeling that comes from achieving your goal.

When I think about the words I chose for values that represent my times of happiness, I feel that I truly do live up to these values. Many of them are ideas or values that my parents attempted to instill in me as a child and things I find personally important within myself and those around me.

I am a very competitive person by nature but I also highly value helping others and making sure that people do not feel left behind. I may always want to finish in first place but I sure do not want to leave anyone on the field struggling. I try to keep an open door policy when it comes to people looking for help and I hope that this attitude carries with me throughout my life. I know that there are days ahead when I will need much help.

Thinking about these values and this exercise reminded me not only of what is important to me but why I chose to come to DBC in the first place. I wanted to chase a dream of mine and even when I get down on myself for not being exactly where I want to be, I know that I am at least following the steps needed to get there.
